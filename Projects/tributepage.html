A. P. J. Abdulkalam

Great INDIAN Scientist and politician who played a leading role in the development of India's missile and nuclear weapons programs

About the legend

-> A.P.J. Abdul kalam, in full Avul pakir jainulabdeen Abdul kalam , was born on october 15,1931, in Rameshwaram,Taminadu,India.

-> He served as 11th president of India from 2002 to 2007.

-> kalam earned a degree in aeronautical engineering from the madras Institute of Technology and in 1958 joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).

-> In 1969,he moved to the Indian space Research Organisation,where he was project director of the the Slv-III,the first satelite launch vehicle that was both designed and produced in India.

->Rejoining DRDO in 1982 , kalam planned the program that produced a number of succesful missiles,which helped earn him the nickname"Missile Man.
-> Among those successes was agni ,India's first Intermediate-rangeballistic missile,which incorporated Intermediate-range ballistic missile ,which incorporated aspects of the SLV-III and was Launched in 1989.

-> He also played a pivotal organisational , technical , and political role in India's pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998,the first since the original nuclear testbby India in 1974.

-> From 1992 to 1997 kalam was scientist adviser to the defence minister , and he later served as principal scientist adviser (1992-2001) to the government with the rank of cabinet minister.

-> His prominent role in the country's 1998 nuclear power and established kalam as a national hero,although the tests caused great concern in the international community.

->In 1998 kalam put forward a countrywide plan called Technology Vision 2020, which he described as a road map for transforming India from as less-developed to a developed society in 20 years . The plan called for, among other measues , incrsasing agricultural productivity ,emphasizing technology as a vehicle for the economic growth, and widening access to health care and education.
